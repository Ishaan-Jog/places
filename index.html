Ellora Caves, Maharashtra

Located in the Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar (formerly Aurangabad) district of Maharashtra, the caves at Ellora, also known as Verul, are a renowned tourist site. The highlight of this site is the magnificent monolithic structure - the Kailasa temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva. According to an inscription, the temple is described as a tall and bright white-colored structure, resembling the Kailasa mountains - the divine abode of Lord Shiva. The Kailasa temple stands as a testament to the advanced engineering of the ancient era.

Hampi, Karnataka

With its mentions dating back to the times of Ramayana, Hampi is an ancient city. It is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Hanuman and the abode of vaanara kings, Vaali and Sugreeva. In the medieval period, Hampi served as the capital of the Vijayanagara empire. Situated on the banks of the Tungabhadra River, the city is adorned with mesmerizing ruins of temples and monuments. When we think of Hampi, the first image that comes to mind is often the Stone Chariot in the Vijaya Vitthala temple complex. It is a must-visit place for all history lovers.

Badami Caves, Karnataka

Built by the Chalukyas in the 5th-6th century, the cave temples at Badami (also known as Vatapi) are an excellent example of ancient cave architecture. The caves are carved out of soft sandstone and feature marvelous stone carvings. These temples are primarily dedicated to Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu. However, some of the last caves are dedicated to Jain Tirthankaras and Lord Buddha. These caves are also one of the oldest in India. Opposite to the caves, we find the Badami fort, which was constructed during a later period.

Ajanta Caves, Maharashtra

Located in Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar (formerly Aurangabad) district of Maharashtra, the rock-cut caves in the village of Ajanta (or Ajintha) house Buddhist, Jain, and Hindu caves. The beautiful wall paintings are the main attraction for tourists. Carved in different time periods, the caves were initiated by the Satvahana dynasty (230 BCE - 220 CE). Additional caves were added by the Vakataka dynasty, Gupta empire, and others, depicting stories from Panchatantra, Jataka tales, and Hindu mythology. There are also Chaityas and Buddhist Viharas.

Kopeshwar Temple, Khidrapur

Located on the banks of the river Krishna at the border of Maharashtra and Karnataka, the ancient Kopeshwar temple in the village of Khidrapur is a marvel. Believed to have been initiated by the Shilahara dynasty in the 11th-12th century, it was later completed or renovated by the Yadava dynasty. Dedicated to Kopeshwara, a form of Lord Shiva, the temple also houses a lingam known as Dhopeshwar representing Lord Vishnu. Don't miss the breathtaking Swarga mandapa, where the full moon aligns perfectly at the center. Adorned with exquisite stone carvings, this temple is a sight to behold.
